Cat Nip Basics

A simple guide to understanding catnip and how it affects cats.

What is Catnip?

Catnip is a fragrant herb from the mint family. Its scientific name is Nepeta cataria. Many cats are attracted to the scent of catnip, and it can trigger playful or relaxed behavior.

How Catnip Works

When cats smell catnip, a natural compound called nepetalactone interacts with receptors in their nose. This can cause a temporary reaction that lasts for several minutes and may include rolling, rubbing, purring, and chasing.

Benefits of Catnip for Cats

  • Encourages exercise and play.
  • Reduces stress and anxiety.
  • Provides a safe form of enrichment.
  • Can make grooming tools and toys more appealing.

Using Catnip Safely

Most cats enjoy catnip without any issues. Offer it in moderation and observe your cat's behavior. A small amount of dried catnip, a toy filled with catnip, or a catnip plant are all suitable options.

Not All Cats React

About 50-70% of cats are sensitive to catnip. Kittens and older cats may not respond, and some cats simply do not exhibit any reaction. Sensitivity is inherited, so it varies by cat.
